Vendor Management & Procurement Portal

1. BACKGROUND
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Ministry of Finance currently manages vendor registrations, procurement approvals,
and contract lifecycle tracking through a combination of spreadsheets, email chains,
and a legacy system that has been out of support since 2021. This results in:

- Duplicate vendor registrations across departments
- Approval bottlenecks averaging 12–18 days per procurement request
- No audit trail for contract amendments
- Manual effort estimated at 3,200 person-hours per year for data entry alone
- Inconsistent vendor qualification records spread across four databases
- No central repository for contract amendments, leading to repeated legal review
- Inability to produce audit-ready reports without 5–7 days of manual consolidation
- No visibility into procurement cycle KPIs for Ministry leadership
- Repeated regulatory findings from the Auditor General's office regarding
  incomplete or missing procurement documentation

The Ministry seeks a modern, web-based Vendor Management & Procurement Portal to
replace these manual processes and serve approximately 400 internal staff users
across 12 departments, plus an unrestricted number of external registered vendors.

2. OBJECTIVES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Ministry of Finance requires the system to:

a) Centralise vendor registration and qualification with a self-service vendor portal
b) Automate multi-stage procurement approval workflows with configurable rules
c) Maintain a complete, immutable audit trail of all procurement decisions
d) Integrate with the Ministry's existing SAP-based financial system for budget checks
e) Provide real-time reporting dashboards for procurement KPIs and spend analytics
f) Comply with PPRA 2024 procurement rules and Government of Pakistan data residency requirements
g) Provide secure, standards-based user authentication, account lifecycle management,
   and self-service password recovery for all user populations
h) Establish a consistent, role-based access control model across all modules
i) Deliver full bilingual support in English and Urdu across every user-facing screen
j) Reduce average procurement cycle time by at least 50% within 12 months of go-live

3. SCOPE OF WORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Vendor Portal (External-Facing)
- Online vendor registration with email verification and CAPTCHA protection
- Vendor profile management (company details, address, banking, tax, contacts)
- Document upload for SECP registration, NTN, financial statements, and ISO certifications
- Document re-upload with version history and automatic expiry detection
- Vendor qualification status tracking with automated notifications
- Re-registration reminder workflows (annual renewal with 90/60/30/7-day reminders)
- Vendor dashboard showing open RFQs/RFPs, bid status, and qualification alerts
- Notification preference centre (email, SMS, in-app)
- Vendor-initiated account deactivation request (subject to Ministry review)
- View of historical bid submissions and outcomes

3.2 Procurement Workflow Engine (Internal)
- Requisition creation and department-level approval routing
- Draft-and-save requisitions with auto-save every 60 seconds
- Reusable requisition templates maintained by each department
- Configurable approval tiers based on procurement value thresholds
  (< PKR 500K: 1-tier; PKR 500K–5M: 2-tier; > PKR 5M: 3-tier + Finance Director sign-off)
- Approver delegation for scheduled leave, with automatic reversion
- Requisition recall and withdrawal before approval completion
- Approval escalation after configurable SLA expiry (default 5 working days)
- RFQ/RFP issuance and bid comparison module
- Sealed bid submission enforcing financial-proposal confidentiality
- Bid evaluation using configurable weighted scoring criteria
- Purchase order generation with SAP integration
- Purchase order amendment workflow with change reason and impact analysis

3.3 Contract Management
- Contract creation from approved POs with template library
- Template version control and approval workflow for template changes
- Milestone and payment schedule tracking
- Bulk milestone import via CSV for large infrastructure contracts
- Amendment history with digital signature support (electronic signature capture)
- Amendment reason codes aligned with PPRA 2024 Article 45
- Contract renewal negotiation workflow with variation tracking
- Contract expiry alerts (90/30/7-day notifications)
- Contract closure checklist (final payment, deliverable confirmation, vendor review)

3.4 Audit & Compliance
- Immutable audit log for all system actions (PPRA Article 44 compliance)
- Audit log integrity verified by cryptographic hash chaining (tamper-evident)
- Role-based access control (Requester, Approver, Finance Officer, Admin, Auditor)
- Conflict-of-interest declaration workflow for approvers
- Export to PDF/Excel for external audit submissions
- Audit log retention minimum 7 years with archival export at retention end
- Mandatory retention-lock: audit logs cannot be edited or deleted by any role
- Per-user access history available to Admin and Auditor roles

3.5 Reporting & Analytics
- Real-time spend analytics dashboard (by department, vendor, category)
- Procurement cycle time metrics
- Vendor performance scorecard
- Custom report builder with scheduled email delivery
- Saved reports with role-based share permissions
- Drill-down from summary to transaction-level detail with one click
- Pinned/favourite widgets per user
- Scheduled distribution lists with opt-out and subscription management
- Export size limit: configurable, default 50,000 rows per export

3.6 User Account & Authentication Management
This is a distinct functional domain and shall be delivered for every user
population (external vendors and all internal roles) with equivalent security
guarantees. The scope below is non-negotiable and applies uniformly.

3.6.1 Registration and Identity Establishment
- Vendor self-service registration with mandatory email verification (link expires
  within 24 hours, single use)
- Vendor registration CAPTCHA and rate limiting (maximum 3 registrations per IP
  per 24 hours) to prevent automated signup abuse
- Internal user accounts provisioned by Ministry IT Admin; no self-service
  registration for internal roles
- First-time login shall force a password change before any other action is allowed
- Mandatory acceptance of Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, and Data Processing Consent
  at registration, with versioned records stored for audit purposes
- Users shall be required to re-accept updated Terms or Privacy Policy versions on
  next login after policy publication

3.6.2 Login and Session Management
- Login using username or verified email plus password as the primary credential
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) mandatory for all internal users; vendors may
  opt into MFA voluntarily but vendor MFA becomes mandatory for bid submission
- MFA methods shall include: Time-based One-Time Password (TOTP) via authenticator
  app (e.g. Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator), email OTP, and SMS OTP
- "Remember this device for 30 days" option using a signed, HTTP-only device token
- Session timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity with a 2-minute warning dialog
  presenting an "Extend Session" button
- Concurrent session limit of 3 per user (configurable by Admin)
- Active session list viewable by the user with remote revoke capability
  ("Sign out all other sessions")
- Logout shall invalidate the current session server-side; "logout everywhere"
  shall invalidate all active sessions for the user
- Failed login attempts shall trigger an email notification to the account
  holder after 3 failed attempts within 15 minutes
- Account lockout after 5 consecutive failed login attempts within 15 minutes;
  lockout duration 15 minutes auto-unlock, or immediate admin unlock via
  Ministry IT helpdesk

3.6.3 Password Recovery and Reset
- "Forgot password" link on login screen initiates a reset workflow
- Reset token delivered via the user's verified email address; token single-use
  with 15-minute expiry
- Alternate SMS-based reset flow available for internal users with a registered
  mobile number
- Reset link shall land on a protected reset page requiring the token and a new
  password conforming to policy
- After reset, all existing sessions for the user shall be invalidated; the user
  shall be required to log in fresh with the new credentials
- Email notification sent to the user after a successful password reset
  ("Your password was changed on <timestamp> from <IP>. If this was not you,
  contact helpdesk immediately.")

3.6.4 In-Session Account Self-Service
- Change password (while authenticated) requires current password, new password,
  and confirmation — new password subject to full policy enforcement
- Change username — subject to uniqueness constraint and, for internal users,
  Admin approval before it takes effect
- Change email — new email must be verified by a confirmation link sent to the
  new address; old email remains active until verification completes
- Change phone number — requires verification via SMS OTP
- Update profile (display name, phone, department, preferred language,
  avatar image up to 2 MB PNG/JPG)
- View own login history (date/time, IP address, device, success/failure)
  for the trailing 12 months
- Self-service account deactivation request — for vendors only; internal users
  must request deactivation through their department head

3.6.5 MFA Lifecycle Management
- Enrol a new MFA factor (TOTP, email, SMS) from the user's security settings
- Set a primary MFA method and one fallback method
- View and revoke enrolled factors (e.g. a lost authenticator device)
- Generate one-time recovery codes (10 codes, single-use each) at MFA enrolment;
  user must acknowledge they have stored them before proceeding
- Regenerate recovery codes, invalidating previously generated codes
- Admin-assisted MFA reset requires identity verification in person or via a
  Ministry-approved video call; reset action is logged in the audit trail

3.6.6 Password Policy and Credential Hygiene
- Minimum 12 characters
- At least one uppercase, one lowercase, one digit, one special character
- Maximum 128 characters
- Disallow the user's username, display name, or email local-part from appearing
  in the password
- Disallow the 10,000 most common passwords (checked against a curated blocklist)
- Password expiry every 90 days (internal users); every 180 days (vendors)
- Password history: cannot reuse last 10 passwords
- Minimum password age 1 day (to prevent rapid cycling to defeat history)
- On-screen password strength meter during password entry
- Password stored using a modern adaptive hash (Argon2id or bcrypt cost ≥ 12);
  never plaintext, never reversibly encrypted

3.6.7 Account Administration (Admin Role)
- Create, view, edit, deactivate, and reactivate internal user accounts
- Assign and reassign roles (Requester, Approver, Finance Officer, Admin, Auditor)
- Unlock locked accounts
- Force password reset on next login for a given user
- Force MFA re-enrolment for a given user
- Revoke all active sessions for a given user
- View system-wide login history, filtered by user, date, success/failure
- Export user directory with role assignments for audit purposes
- Admin actions on user accounts are irrevocably logged in the audit trail with
  the acting Admin's identity and the target user's identifier

5. TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Hosting
- System must be hosted on Government of Pakistan approved on-premise infrastructure
  or a CLOUD-PKI compliant cloud provider with data residency in Pakistan
- Vendors must not have access to government data stored on external servers
- Disaster recovery site shall be geographically separated from the primary site
  by at least 100 km, with RPO ≤ 1 hour and RTO ≤ 4 hours
- Environment separation: development, staging/UAT, and production environments
  shall be physically and logically isolated with independent access controls

5.2 Security
- Multi-factor authentication mandatory for all internal users
- End-to-end encryption for document uploads and contract storage
- Penetration testing report required before go-live; all critical and high
  findings remediated
- OWASP Top 10 (2021) compliance mandatory across web and API surfaces
- OWASP API Security Top 10 compliance mandatory for all exposed APIs
- All passwords hashed with Argon2id or bcrypt cost ≥ 12
- All sessions bound to client IP and user agent; session cookies HTTP-only,
  Secure, SameSite=Lax
- CSRF protection on all state-changing endpoints via synchronizer tokens
- Content Security Policy header enforced with strict default-src
- HTTP Strict Transport Security with min-age ≥ 1 year
- Protection against account enumeration: identical user-facing responses for
  existent and non-existent usernames/emails on login and reset flows
- Rate limiting: login 5 req/min/IP, password reset 3 req/hour/account,
  API 100 req/min per authenticated user, 20 req/min per unauthenticated client

5.3 Integration
- SAP ECC 6.0 integration for budget availability checks and PO posting
- National Identity Database (NADRA) API for vendor NIC verification
- Pakistan Post e-stamp API for digital contract stamping
- Email/SMS notifications via SMTP and SMS gateway
- OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect support for potential future SSO integration with
  the Federal Government Enterprise IAM platform (not in current scope but
  must not be architecturally precluded)

5.4 Performance
- System must support 400 concurrent users with < 3 second page load times at p95
- Search operations shall return results in < 2 seconds at p95 under full load
- 99.5% uptime SLA required during government working hours (8am–6pm PKT)
- Document storage capacity: minimum 10TB expandable
- Login throughput: sustained 50 logins/second with < 1 second response

5.5 Accessibility and Usability
- Bilingual interface (English and Urdu) across every user-facing screen,
  including authentication flows
- RTL layout correctness for Urdu
- WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance for all user-facing interfaces
- Keyboard-only navigation for all workflows including login and MFA
- Screen reader support for labels, form errors, and dynamic content

5.6 Browser and Device Support
- Current and immediately preceding major versions of Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari
- Responsive layout for desktop, tablet, and mobile viewports
- No ActiveX, Flash, Java applet, or browser plugin dependencies

6. BUSINESS RULES (INDICATIVE)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

BR-01  No vendor may receive an RFQ or RFP unless their qualification status is "Qualified" and not expired
BR-02  An approver may not approve a requisition in which they or a first-degree relative have a declared interest
BR-03  Requisitions above PKR 5,000,000 require Finance Director sign-off in addition to department approval tiers
BR-04  Contract amendments that increase total value by more than 15% require re-approval at the original approval tier
BR-05  Audit log entries are immutable; any attempt to modify or delete shall be rejected and logged as a security event
BR-06  Vendor re-registration must be completed annually; bid submission is disabled after expiry until re-registration is accepted
BR-07  A user may have only one active primary MFA method at a time
BR-08  Password reset tokens are single-use and expire 15 minutes after issuance
BR-09  Deactivated user accounts retain their audit history permanently
BR-10  Display of any monetary value shall include the currency code and use thousands separators per Pakistani convention
